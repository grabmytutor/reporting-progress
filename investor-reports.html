The platform thesis

  • Vertical SaaS for tuition centres — EdBuddies powers billing, scheduling, attendance, and parent-teacher communication for K-12 enrichment centres in Southeast Asia.
  • Predictable transaction engine — 96.6% of T12M volume is recurring monthly auto-billing, not project-based.

Platform Take-Rate Scenarios (what-if EdBuddies charges 1–3% per transaction)

Today, EdBuddies operates as a SaaS for centres without a transaction fee. A 1–3% take-rate on platform GMV is a standard vertical-platform monetisation lever that compounds with growth and is invisible to centres at industry-low rates. Take-rate = % of transaction value retained by the platform.

Baseline T12M GMV per active centre = SGD 90,307 (T12M GMV ÷ 6 centres). Growth multiplier scales the active-centre count linearly; in practice growth would also lift ARPU per centre.

Why take-rate is the right lever for EdBuddies

  • It scales with success — the more a centre grows, the more EdBuddies earns; aligned incentives.
  • It is invisible at low rates — 1–2% is below typical card-processor pricing; centres don't feel it as a tax.
  • It compounds with the activation funnel — every dormant account that lights up immediately yields take-rate revenue.
  • It is recurring by construction — 96.6% of platform GMV is auto-billed monthly subscriptions, so platform-fee revenue inherits that predictability.
  • It does not cannibalise existing SaaS revenue — centres continue paying their software fee; the take-rate is layered on top of payment processing.
