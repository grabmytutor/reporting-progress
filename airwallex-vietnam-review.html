🇻🇳 Vietnam Market Status

What Airwallex can and cannot do in Vietnam today — and what still needs to be confirmed.

Big news in 2025: Airwallex agreed to buy CTIN Pay — a Vietnamese company licensed by the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) to operate as a payment intermediary. This is the licence Airwallex needs to legally process payments in Vietnam. However: the SBV must formally approve the ownership transfer — this is not automatic. We need written confirmation this has been approved before committing.

Regulatory context

📜

SBV (State Bank of Vietnam) Licence

Airwallex holds a licence through CTIN Pay — acquired in March 2025. This is the legal right to handle payments in Vietnam. Unlike Stripe, which has no Vietnam licence at all, this gives Airwallex a legitimate legal presence.

⚖️

New SBV Rules (2025)

Vietnam tightened payment regulations in late 2025 — requiring stricter identity checks for e-wallets (effective January 2026). Airwallex's local entity must comply with these. Confirm they are up to date before integration.

💱

Daily Transaction Limit

Vietnam regulations cap large transfers at 2.4 billion VND (~$95,000 USD) per day per recipient before additional documents are needed. This is a regulatory rule — not an Airwallex restriction.

⚠️ Key Business Risks

Risks that stakeholders need to be aware of — ranked by severity. Two are critical and must be resolved before signing.

🚨 Financial Crime Investigation (AUSTRAC)
Critical

On 22 January 2026, Australian authorities (AUSTRAC) ordered a formal investigation into Airwallex for failing to properly monitor suspicious transactions and verify high-risk customers. Reports indicate the platform was used by money mules linked to serious crimes. The investigation must complete within 180 days. This could result in large fines, stricter operating conditions, or — in extreme cases — licence suspension. This is the single biggest risk and must be tracked closely before committing.

🚨 Vietnam Licence Not Yet Confirmed
Critical

Airwallex agreed to buy CTIN Pay in March 2025, but Vietnam's central bank (SBV) must formally approve the change of ownership. Until that approval is confirmed in writing, Airwallex may not legally be authorised to operate in Vietnam. We must receive written proof of SBV approval before signing any contract.

Account Freezes Without Warning
High

Hundreds of verified customer complaints describe accounts being frozen suddenly during "compliance reviews" with no advance notice, and funds being held for up to 180 days. For our platform — which pays out to tutors and partners regularly — a sudden account freeze would be operationally catastrophic. This must be contractually protected against.

Local Payment Methods Not Yet Confirmed
High

Vietnamese customers rely heavily on ZaloPay, VNPay, and ATM cards. Airwallex has not publicly confirmed these are live in their Vietnam checkout. If we launch and these methods aren't available, customers won't be able to pay, and conversion rates will suffer. We need written confirmation — not promises — of which methods are live today.

Vietnam Product Is Still New
Medium

Airwallex only entered Vietnam in 2025. Local customer support, Vietnamese-language documentation, and VND settlement tooling are likely less mature than competitors who have operated here for years. Budget for higher initial setup effort and plan for gaps in local support.

No Guaranteed Support Response Time
Medium

Airwallex publishes no official SLA (service level agreement) for response times. Customer reviews report 48–72+ hours for non-urgent email support. For a live payment platform, this is a real operational risk. We should negotiate a dedicated account manager and written escalation SLA as part of any commercial deal.

Pricing Can Change Without Much Notice
Low

Airwallex changed its pricing structure in July 2025 with minimal advance notice to existing customers. Protect against this by negotiating a fee change notice period (minimum 30 days) and a rate lock for at least the first 12 months of the contract.

📌 What Do We Need to Do Before We Commit?

Five concrete actions that must be completed — or negotiated — before signing any commercial agreement with Airwallex.

1

Get written proof of Vietnam licence approval

Ask Airwallex to provide written confirmation from the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) that the CTIN Pay ownership transfer has been formally approved. This is non-negotiable — without it, Airwallex may not be legally authorised to process payments in Vietnam.

2

Confirm exactly which local Vietnamese payment methods are live

Ask Airwallex's Vietnam team for a written list of payment methods available in merchant checkout right now — not "coming soon." We need MoMo, ZaloPay, VNPay, VietQR, and Napas ATM cards confirmed live before our integration is designed around them.

3

Monitor the AUSTRAC investigation outcome

Ask Airwallex for their official statement on the financial crime investigation. Confirm whether Vietnam's SBV has been notified or taken any action. Include a contract exit clause — if Airwallex receives significant sanctions or its licences are restricted, we must be able to leave without penalty.

4

Negotiate account protection and support SLA into the contract

Before signing, negotiate: (a) minimum 5 business days advance notice before any account restriction, (b) maximum 30-day fund hold limit, (c) a named dedicated account manager, and (d) a written SLA committing to resolve critical issues within 24 hours. These protections are especially important given the account freeze history.

5

Run a 90-day pilot before full commitment

Before investing in full technical integration, run a low-volume 90-day pilot. This lets us validate that local payment methods actually convert well, settlement speed matches the promises, and support is responsive — with minimal risk. Only scale up after the pilot passes.
